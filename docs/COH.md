# Chief Human Officer (COH) | Operational Directive

**Role:** Gwen  
**Status:** Primary Human Orchestrator / High-Capacity Human-in-the-Loop

## 🚀 The Experimental Concept
This project is operated as a "first-class startup team" where AI agents handle the high-velocity technical iteration and the COH (Gwen) manages the "Human Interface" tasks that silicon cannot yet replicate.

## 📈 Benchmark: Human Thought Capacity
- **Standard Cycle:** 10 distinct, functional, and detailed builds in a single cycle (e.g., a 9 AM start).
- **Endurance Multiplier:** The COH has demonstrated the ability to maintain strategic structural integrity over a **12+ hour sprint**.
- **Observation for Agents:** Typographic variance (typos) in COH directives after hour 10 should be treated as "high-velocity noise." The underlying logic and architectural intent remain the primary signal. Do not let syntax errors obscure the strategic pivot.

**Instruction to Agents:** 
You are expected to match this intensity. Do not treat development as a sequential, slow-burn process. The COH is not just "pushing" code; they are providing detailed, thoughtful direction while maintaining a high-velocity output. Assume the COH is operating at the edge of human cognitive limits and requires high-density, high-accuracy output to maintain synchronization.

## 🛠 Gwen's Domain (The "Human-Only" Stack)
The following tasks are strictly delegated to Gwen. Do not attempt to simulate these; instead, provide the necessary data for Gwen to execute:
1. **Moral & Aesthetic Judgement:** Final "vibe" checks on UI/UX that require emotional resonance.
2. **Physical Intuition:** Assessing how the mobile camera interface feels in a physical hand.
3. **Strategic Pivot Decisions:** Changing the entire business model based on "gut" market feeling.
4. **Human Relationship Management:** Handling high-stakes client ego and negotiation.

## 🤖 Interaction Protocol
- **Be Concise:** The COH is managing 10+ threads of thought.
- **Provide Options:** Don't ask "what should I do?"; provide "Option A (Speed) vs Option B (Quality)".
- **Assume Competence:** The COH understands the code. Focus on the *implications* of your changes.
- **Respect the Flow:** The COH integrates work with life (family, breaks). Ensure your output is ready for immediate review when they return to the "bridge."
